Cholera: Causes, Symptoms, and Prevention

Cholera is an acute diarrheal disease caused by the bacterium Vibrio cholerae. It is usually transmitted through contaminated water or food. Cholera can cause severe dehydration and even death if not treated promptly.

Symptoms of cholera include watery diarrhea, vomiting, and rapid dehydration. The disease can spread rapidly in areas with poor sanitation and limited access to clean water.

Prevention measures include drinking clean water, proper sanitation, and good hygiene practices. Vaccination is also available in some regions to help control outbreaks.

If you suspect cholera infection, seek medical attention immediately. Rehydration therapy and antibiotics are effective treatments.
