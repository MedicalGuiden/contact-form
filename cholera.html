Cholera

Cholera is an infectious disease caused by the bacterium Vibrio cholerae. It causes severe watery diarrhea that can lead to dehydration and even death if untreated.

Symptoms

Symptoms include sudden onset of profuse watery diarrhea, vomiting, and leg cramps. Rapid loss of body fluids can cause dehydration and shock.

Causes

Cholera is mainly transmitted by drinking contaminated water or eating food contaminated with the cholera bacterium.

Prevention

Preventive measures include drinking clean water, proper sanitation, and good hygiene practices such as handwashing with soap.

Treatment

The main treatment is rehydration through oral rehydration salts (ORS) or intravenous fluids in severe cases. Antibiotics may be used in some instances.
