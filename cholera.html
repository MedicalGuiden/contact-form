Cholera

Cholera is an acute diarrheal illness caused by infection of the intestine with Vibrio cholerae bacteria. It is often linked to contaminated water or food. Common symptoms include watery diarrhea, dehydration, and muscle cramps. If left untreated, cholera can be fatal within hours.

Prevention includes proper sanitation, clean drinking water, and good hygiene. In areas with risk of outbreaks,vaccination is also recommended. If infected, treatment with oral rehydration salts (ORS) and fluids is essential.
