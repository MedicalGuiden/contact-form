Cholera: Causes, Symptoms & Treatment

Cholera is an acute diarrheal disease caused by the bacterium Vibrio cholerae. It spreads through contaminated water or food and can lead to severe dehydration if untreated.

Symptoms

  • Watery diarrhea
  • Vomiting
  • Leg cramps
  • Rapid heart rate
  • Loss of skin elasticity

Causes

Contaminated water and poor sanitation are the main sources. Outbreaks are common in areas with limited access to clean water.

Prevention

  • Drink clean, boiled, or treated water
  • Wash hands regularly
  • Eat thoroughly cooked food

Treatment

Oral rehydration salts (ORS), intravenous fluids, and antibiotics in severe cases. Early treatment drastically reduces fatality.
